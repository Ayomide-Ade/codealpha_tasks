Phishing Awareness Training

Learn to identify, avoid, and report cyber threats.

1. What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cybercrime in which a target is contacted by email, telephone, or text message by someone posing as a legitimate institution. Their goal is to lure individuals into providing sensitive data such as personally identifiable information, banking details, and passwords.

Social Engineering Tactics: Attackers manipulate human psychology. They often create a false sense of urgency (e.g., "Your account will be suspended in 24 hours") or curiosity to force you into making a quick, unthinking decision.

2. Recognizing Fake Emails & Websites

Attackers are getting smarter, but they usually leave clues. Always look for these red flags:

  • Mismatched Domains: The email might say it is from "PayPal," but the sender address is security@paypa1-update.com.
  • Generic Greetings: Using "Dear Customer" instead of your actual name.
  • Suspicious Links: Hover over a link before clicking. If the destination URL looks strange or uses a URL shortener, do not click it.
  • Poor Spelling and Grammar: Professional organizations rarely make glaring typos in official communications.

Real-World Example

Subject: URGENT: Verify Your Bank Details Now!

Dear Customer,

We have detected unusual login activity on your account. Please click the link below immediately to verify your identity, otherwise your account will be permanently locked.

http://www.secure-login-bank-update.xyz

Thank you,
The Security Team

3. Best Practices for Prevention

  • Verify the Source: If you receive a suspicious email from a colleague or bank, contact them directly using a known, trusted phone number or website.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Even if a phisher gets your password, MFA provides an extra layer of security.
  • Keep Software Updated: Ensure your operating system and browser have the latest security patches.
  • Report It: Do not just delete the email. Report it to your IT department or email provider to help protect others.
